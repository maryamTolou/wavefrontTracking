VTrack

VTrack is used for tracking the wavefront on the heart, and finding the anomalies in movements of this wave which leads us to detect the origins of Ventricular tachycardia (VT). VTrack, is a two step method. In the first step we track the wavefront by using mathematical trasnformations, and in the second step we locate the anomalies with clustering.

As shown in the left image in figure one, the input to our tracking method are two consecutive wavefront movement frames. The trasnformer model will then transform the first image to the second image and output the transformed image and the transformation matrix itself. The transformation matrix can be considered as tracking movement from time-step 1 in first frame and the second time-step in the next frame. To illustrate, if we are able to track the wavefront, we can easily observe the anomalous behaviour around the channel and detect the VT origins. Finding the transformation of consecutive frames in wave movement video is exactly how we track the wavefront.

As shown in the right image in figure 1, the second portion of VTrack is used for anomaly detection. We classify the transformations in two classes of normal movement and abnormal movement. The goal is that the abnormal wave movements around the VT curcuit will be captured in the abnormal cluster.

figure 1: This image shows the architecture of VTrack. The image on the left shows our tracking method using mathematical transformations and the image on the right shows our anomaly detection with clustering.
