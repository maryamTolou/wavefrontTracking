Experiments and results

Data:

The data we applied our model on, is based on a simulation data of a 3d heart mesh and the wavefront matrix recorded for 273 time-steps and over five heart beats. In the three of these beats the VT occurs and for seconds, we are able to see the anomalous behaviour of the wavefront movement around the circuit. We visualized the wavefront on the 3D mesh of the heart using PARAVIEW software and exported the results as static frames from the wavefront movement video. We converted the images to gray-scale images because our experiments showed that our model is not able to provide accurate transformations on the RGB images.

Tracking with transformation:

The input to this part of our model are pairs of consecutive frames of the data explained in previous section. This model will output the transformation matrix together with the transformed image. We output the transformed image to be able to understand how well our model is applying the transformations. The images below show a couple examples of our tracking/trasnformaion results. In each figure, the image on the left is the source image, the middle image is the destination, and the image on the right is the result of transforming the first image to the second one.

As you can see in the above images, the transformed image is getting close to the destination image and by observing the results over many samples, we noticed that we can say that our method can fairly track the wave movement.

Anomaly detection results:

After finding the transformations among all pairs of consecutive frames, as explained in the previous section, we then used KNN to cluster the transformations in two groups of normal and abnormal cluster. In the figures below we are showcasing 4 examples of data considered in normal and abnormal cluster. We compared these results with our groundtruth, the video of wavefront movement, and noticed that about 80% of the data categorized in the abnormal cluster, were really related to the VT origins. Since these results are initial results, we beleive that this method is promissing and tracking the wavefront based on transformation can be considered as a novel way of detecting VT.
