Experiments and results

Data:

The data we applied our model on, is based on asimulation data of a 3d heart and the wavefront matrix recorded for 273 time-steps and over five heart beats. In the three of these beats the VT occurs and for seconds, we are able to see the anomalous behavour of the wavefront movement around the circuit. We visualized the wavefront on the 3D mesh of the heart using PARAVIEW software and exported the results as static frames from the wavefront movement video. We converted the images to gray-scale images because our experiments showed that our model is not able to provide accurate transformations on the RGB images.

Tracking with transformation:

The input to this part of our model are two consecutive frames of the data explained in previous section. This model will output the transformation matrox together with the transformed image. We output the transformed image to be able to understand how well our model is applying the transformations. The images below show a couple examples of our tracking/trasnformaion results. In each figure, the image on the left is the source image, the middle image is the destination, and the image on the right is transformation result of the first image to the second one.
